Featured Projects

1. Internship at Vogue Australia

I explored the clean-girl aesthetic as a cultural "norm" and published my voice, tracing how effort shapes the idea of effortlessness. Working closely with Gladys Lai and Nina Miyashita taught me precision, pace, and cultural fluency. I valued the daily editorial discussions and learning the full spectrum of fashion journalism: from rigorous pitching to commercial work.

2. Upcycled!

I grew up making clothes from scratch and rags for my barbie doll, and by high school, I apply the similar materials onto myself. They were old clothes, clothes passed down by my sister that were so worn out, and some secondary school uniform I'd never wear again. This hobby is spending late nights to sew, drape, design and problem solve for some fun fashion. I was bad at documenting, so these are 12 pieces that fortunately captured.

3. Patagonia Data Illustration Internship

10 minutes after my Sustainability in Fashion class, still emotional about Patagonia's contribution to society, I got an email: a job for DCDA students, creating StoryMaps for Patagonia using ESRI. I was learning about ESRI in Geospatial Tech class 2 hours ago. Excited, I looked up, my Sustainability in Fashion professor happened to walk by. It felt like fate. I became the sophomore fashion student working with 3 incredible Geography seniors: Ainsley Elliott, Olivia Dugger, and Carolyn Kennett, under the guidance of Sean Crotty & Kyle Walker, building data-driven storytelling web apps that are published. See more about the project (Rianne said I'm a senior, but I'm not)

4. Multicultural Fashion Show

On the 1st day of my first Winter Break, I got a call from a senior theatre major, Mikayla, asking me, the only Vietnamese Fashion Merchandising freshman, to help with putting together a fashion show to represent the diverse backgrounds of the minority non-caucasion community. We came up with the concept of unifying our nuanced cultures under the 4 elemental theme, to bridge gaps, so juxtapose, and to work together. I develop the concepts, designed, styled, and coordinated the models, and modeled myself in front of 300+ attendees.

5. 3D Media: Surrealism & Simulation

For this project, I worked with Blender, Unreal Engine, 3D printing machines, and Davinci Resolve. I eventually found my understanding of spaces and digital interactions significantly broadened, as I observe myself navigating the 3D environment and others interactions to my artworks.
